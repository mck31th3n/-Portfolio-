AI Governance Systems

The Global AI Capability Floor

AI is becoming civilization-level infrastructure, but current governance debates often collapse into a narrow choice between acceleration, moratorium, or self-regulation. This proposal explores a third path: a treaty-bound capability floor that accepts frontier AI advantage as a reality while rejecting permanent AI exclusion as an acceptable global outcome. The framework proposes delayed access to audited, prior-generation civilian AI capability through governed international pools, tiered use categories, conditional accountability, independent audit capacity, and escalation rules for verified hostile use. The public case study focuses on the system architecture — how to balance safety, sovereignty, access, and accountability.

Demonstrates
AI policy design, global infrastructure thinking, safety/access tradeoff analysis, institutional architecture, governance workflow design.
Status

Platform Labor Systems

Reliability-Gated Dasher Earnings Floor

Gig platforms typically optimize for order coverage while drivers absorb the volatility of demand swings, dead zones, and unpaid waiting. This concept proposes a narrow, opt-in alternative: a time-and-location-scoped earnings floor triggered only during operationally-justified coverage gaps, tiered by driver standing, with real fraud/abuse controls and a legally-sound early-exit mechanism that avoids retroactive pay clawback.

Demonstrates
Marketplace incentive design, labor-platform governance, cost/abuse-control modeling, operational fairness under an independent-contractor legal structure.
Status

Unsolicited concept proposal. Not submitted to, reviewed by, or endorsed by DoorDash. No pilot has been run.

Creative AI Systems

Consent-Based Reference Rails for AI Music Platforms

AI music platforms often treat artist references as a blunt restriction problem: block the prompt, hide the reasoning, or push users into coded language. This concept explores a more structured alternative: keep broad musical descriptors open, while routing explicit named artist, project, or song references through opt-in licensed pathways with clear user review, reference scope, and lineage tracking. The concept also explores a lightweight independent-artist discovery lane, where artists can voluntarily make selected style references available for visibility and usage analytics rather than immediate licensing revenue.

Demonstrates
Creative AI governance, rights-aware UX, consent-based workflow design, creator-platform incentive modeling.
Status

Early concept exploration. Not submitted to, reviewed by, or endorsed by Suno. No legal, rights-holder, or platform validation has been conducted.

EXECUTIVE FUNCTION

Speculative Governance Simulation

Project Overview

Executive Function is an experimental AI-driven governance simulation that models presidential-level decision-making under crisis conditions. The system explores how multiple AI advisors with competing priorities navigate complex geopolitical scenarios.

Core Concept

Users assume the role of a head of state and receive counsel from four distinct AI advisors, each representing different governmental perspectives:

  • Alpha Advisor - Military & national security strategy
  • Beta Advisor - Economic policy and fiscal consequences
  • Charlie Advisor - Diplomatic relations and international law
  • Delta Advisor - Humanitarian impact and ethical considerations

Technical Implementation

The simulation presents real historical crisis scenarios (2009 financial collapse, COVID-19 response, etc.) and generates multi-perspective analysis from each advisor. Users make decisions and receive consequence modeling showing ripple effects across military, economic, diplomatic, and humanitarian dimensions.

Research Goals

Exploring AI's capacity for multi-stakeholder perspective-taking, ethical complexity modeling, and consequence forecasting. The project investigates whether AI can help humans understand the interconnected nature of high-stakes governance decisions.

Design Philosophy

Built with a "presidential command center" aesthetic emphasizing gravity and responsibility. The interface deliberately creates cognitive friction to slow decision-making and encourage thoughtful consideration of competing priorities.
